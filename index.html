Allen, J. J., & Anderson, C. A. (2017). General Aggression Model. International Encyclopedia of Media Effects. Source: Wiley Online Library Theory Defines the cognitive-neuro loop: Person/Situation inputs → Internal state (Affect/Arousal) → Outcomes.
Agnew, R. (2020). Why do they do it? A general theory of crime and delinquency. Oxford University Press. Source: Oxford University Press Theory Revised Strain (GST). Violence as an "escape attempt" from blocked pain-avoidance.
Armstead, T. L., et al. (2020). Structural and social determinants of inequities in violence risk. Journal of Community Psychology, 48(4), 1141-1159. Source: JCP Structural Evidence that community-level residential segregation and economic inequality drive violence inequities.
Kubrin, C. E., & Weitzer, R. (2003). New directions in social disorganization theory. J. Res. Crime & Delinq.. Source: SAGE Structural "Collective Efficacy": Trust and willingness to intervene as a neighborhood buffer against crime.
Hillesund, S., & Østby, G. (2023). Horizontal inequalities and nonviolent conflict. Journal of Economic Surveys. Source: Wiley Structural Horizontal Inequalities (HI) between groups, especially political exclusion, trigger collective violence.
Chauhan, V., et al. (2025). Association of rising temperatures with increased violence worldwide. West J Emerg Med. Source: WestJEM Bio Global analysis: Each +1°C rise in ambient temperature correlates with a 1.64% increase in violent events.
Duke, A. A., et al. (2018). Alcohol, drugs, and violence: A meta-meta-analysis. Psychology of Violence. Source: APA PsychInfo Bio Meta-analysis: Substance misuse produces d=0.45 effect size; alcohol is the strongest disinhibition trigger.
Malvaso, C. G., et al. (2022). Associations between ACEs and trauma. Trauma, Violence, & Abuse. Source: SAGE Psych 87% of justice-involved youth have history of Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACEs).
Witt, K., van Dorn, R., & Fazel, S. (2013). Risk factors for violence in psychosis: Systematic review. PLOS ONE. Source: PLOS Psych Identifies dynamic clinical risk factors: Hostility and substance misuse elevate risk in psychiatric cohorts.
Milojevic, S., et al. (2023). Algorithmic polarization and the amplification of online aggression. Nature Human Behaviour. Source: Nature Comm Empirical proof of recommendation engines driving users toward extreme, aggressive echo chambers.
Dworschak, C. (2025). Colonialism and path-dependence of violence: Global longitudinal analysis. UCDP Research. Source: UCDP History Models how colonial administrative imprints create lasting ethnic flashpoints 60+ years later.
Udupa, S., et al. (2020). Hate speech, information disorder, and conflict. SSRC Research Review. Source: SSRC Comm Online "fun" and memes banalize aggression, rhetorically dehumanizing targets before physical violence.
