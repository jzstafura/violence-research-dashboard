Hillesund, S., & Østby, G. (2023). Horizontal inequalities and nonviolent conflict. Journal of Economic Surveys, 37(3), 883-909. View Full Article (Wiley) Structural Horizontal Inequalities (HI) between groups, especially political exclusion, are primary drivers of civil war.
Chauhan, V., et al. (2025). Association of rising temperatures with increased violence worldwide: A systematic review. Western Journal of Emergency Medicine. View Journal Site (WestJEM) Bio Confirms +1°C rise in temp = 1.64% increase in violent events; zero significant effect on property crimes.
Duke, A. A., et al. (2018). Alcohol, drugs, and violence: A meta-meta-analysis. Psychology of Violence, 8(2), 238–249. View Full Article (APA) Bio Large-scale meta-analysis: Substance misuse correlates with violence across clinical and non-clinical populations.
Malvaso, C. G., et al. (2022). The prevalence of adverse childhood experiences among justice-involved youth: A systematic review. Trauma, Violence, & Abuse, 23(4). View Full Article (SAGE) Psych Synthesizes the 87% ACEs prevalence finding among justice-involved youth populations.
Whiting, D., et al. (2020). Violence and mental disorders. The Lancet Psychiatry, 7(1), 93-102. View Full Article (Lancet) Psych Clinical risk audit: Mental illness increases relative risk, but absolute violence rates remain low without substance misuse.

Udupa, S., et al. (2020). Hate speech, information disorder, and conflict. SSRC Research Review. View Full Article (SSRC) Comm Analyzes online "fun" and memes as catalysts for dehumanizing discourse and collective aggression.
Kollmeyer, C. (2025). Colonial legacy and contemporary civil violence. Social Forces. View Journal (Oxford/Social Forces) History Analyzes path-dependent imprints of colonial administrative styles on modern civil violence patterns.
